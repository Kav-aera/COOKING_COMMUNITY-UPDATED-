Mushroom Risotto

How to Make Mushroom Risotto
"Indulge in the rich and comforting flavors of our Mushroom Risotto, a classic Italian dish that marries the earthy essence of sautéed mushrooms with creamy Arborio rice. This savory masterpiece is the perfect balance of simplicity and elegance, making it an ideal choice for a heartwarming dinner."

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : meals risotto food

instructions

step 1 : PREPARE THE MUSHROOMS

In a large skillet or wide saucepan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced mushrooms and sauté until they are browned and any liquid released has evaporated. Remove the mushrooms from the pan and set them aside.

step 2: SAUTE THE ONIONS AND GARLIC

In the same pan, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of olive oil and 1 tablespoon of butter. Add the chopped onion and sauté for a few minutes until it becomes translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional 30 seconds.

step 3: TOAST THE RICE

Add the Arborio rice to the pan with the onions and garlic. Stir to coat the rice with the oil and cook for about 2 minutes until the edges of the rice grains turn translucent.

step 4: DEGLAZE THE WINE AND COOK RISOTTO

If using wine, pour it into the pan and stir until it is mostly absorbed by the rice. Begin adding the vegetable broth, one ladleful at a time. Stir continuously and allow the liquid to be absorbed before adding more. Continue this process until the rice is cooked, which should take about 18-20 minutes. The risotto should be creamy with a slight bite to the rice (al dente).

ingredients

1 1/2 cups Arborio rice

1 small onion, finely chopped

2 cloves garlic, minced

4 cups vegetable broth (approximately)

1/2 cup dry white wine (optional)

1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

2 tablespoons olive oil

2 tablespoons unsalted butter

tools

Large Skillet or Wide Saucepan

Grater

Ladle

Vegetable Broth
