Crepes with ham and cheese

How to Make Crepes with hama nd cheese
"Indulge in a delightful morning with our savory Crepes with Ham and Cheese â€“ a perfect blend of thin, tender crepes embracing layers of thinly sliced ham and melted cheese. The ultimate breakfast treat that's quick to make and even quicker to disappear!"

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : breakfast non-veg food

instructions

step 1 : PREPARE THE CREPE BATTER

In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and a pinch of salt. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, and melted butter. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, whisking until you have a smooth batter. It should be similar in consistency to heavy cream. If it's too thick, you can add a bit more milk.

step 2: LET THE BATTER REST

Allow the crepe batter to rest for at least 30 minutes. This allows the flour to fully hydrate and results in a smoother, tender crepe.

step 3: COOK THE CREPES

Heat a crepe pan or non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add a small amount of cooking spray or butter to grease the pan. Pour a ladleful of crepe batter into the pan, swirling it around to coat the bottom evenly. You want to make a thin crepe. Cook for about 1-2 minutes until the edges start to lift and the bottom is lightly browned. Use a spatula to carefully flip the crepe and cook the other side for another 1-2 minutes. Remove the cooked crepe and repeat the process with the remaining batter.

step 4: ASSEMBLE THE CREPES

Lay a crepe on a flat surface or plate. Place a few slices of ham on one half of the crepe. Sprinkle grated cheese over the ham. Optionally, sprinkle some fresh herbs over the cheese for added flavor.

step 5: FOLD AND SERVE

Fold the other half of the crepe over the filling, creating a half-moon shape. You can serve the crepes as is, or if you'd like, briefly warm them in the pan to melt the cheese.

ingredients

1 cup all-purpose flour

2 large eggs

1 1/2 cups milk

2 tablespoons melted butter

A pinch of salt

Cooking spray or additional butter for the pan

tools

Mixing bowls

Whisk

Crepe pan or non-stick skillet

Ladle or measuring cup

Spatula

Grater (for the cheese)

Plate or platter for serving
